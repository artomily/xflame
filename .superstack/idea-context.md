### Target Market (revised after research)

Initial target was generic "retail/individual," which failed the crypto-necessity test — self-custody isn't a draw for mainstream retail who can just use Bibit/OVO/bank auto-invest. Revised target: **families in Indonesia/SEA receiving stablecoin remittances from migrant workers abroad**, where:
- Indonesia remittance market ~USD 18B, ~4.5M migrant workers abroad, ~USD 10.6B/year inbound (Ken Research, World Bank)
- Indonesia + Philippines + Vietnam + Thailand combined >USD 70B/year inbound remittances
- MoneyGram (MGUSD) and Velo Labs/Lightnet already building stablecoin remittance settlement on Stellar — reduces the "build trust from zero" problem since rails/trust are being established by others
- 50M+ unbanked/underbanked in Indonesia; bank transfers cost up to 8% and take days vs. stablecoin <1% and minutes

### Split Rule Variants (product mechanic)

1. **Fixed split** — static % to stability vault / DCA basket / cash (default onboarding)
2. **Smart split** — DCA allocation scales reactively with price moves (needs on-chain oracle)
3. **Goal-based split** — named "pockets" (dana darurat, DP motor, etc.) each with target + vault, auto-redirect once a goal is met

### Why Not Web2

Auto-invest features exist in Web2 (Acorns, Qapital, Bibit) but are custodial and don't solve the actual pain of THIS segment: stablecoin income that never touches a bank, remittance settlement fees of 5-8% via traditional rails, and unbanked recipients. Removing the blockchain removes the reason the product can auto-split the instant money lands, for near-zero cost, without a bank intermediary.
